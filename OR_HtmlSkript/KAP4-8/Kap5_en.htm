5. Sensitivity analysis

The objective of the sensitivity analysis is to analyse possible variations for the individual coefficients of the LP model, maintaining the optimum in its quality.
As it is not sensible to allow for the coefficient of the A matrix to vary (it is better to calculate with a new model!), we can concentrate on the

  • variation of a coefficient of the objective function, and the
  • variation of a coefficient of the b vector.

In other words, we concentrate on the quantitative transformation of the optimal tableau.


If in such "post-optimal" analyses, we are interested in the modification of the optimal tableau beyond the variations that have occurred with the sensibility analysis and with data constellations that are dependent on each other, we employ the parametric programming.

5.2 Variation of a coefficient of the objective function

For the dual variables applies (see 4):

The dual variables are also subject to the non-negativity restriction!
(Only applies when the basic variables in the optimal tableau are equivalent to the structure variables!)

It has to apply:

Example for the standard model:

   with c2 = 2:   u1 =  3/7 cl - 2 * 1/7 ≥ 0;
                  u2 = -2/7 cl + 2 * 3/7 ≥ 0;

This results in: cl 2/3 and cl 3

In other words: if cl exceeds the value of 3, a basis change occurs. If cl is between 2/3 and 3, the optimal solution remains its "quality", i.e. the basis remains unchanged.

(Analogous for c2!)


5.3 Variation of a coefficient of the b vector

For the optimal solution applies:

(Non-negativity restriction!)

For the standard example this results in:

   3/7 bl - 9 * 2/7 ≥ 0;
  -1/7 bl + 9 * 3/7 ≥ 0;

with b2 = 9;
bl 6 and bl 27.

In other words: if the value of bl is between 6 and 27, the basis of the optimal solution does not change!
