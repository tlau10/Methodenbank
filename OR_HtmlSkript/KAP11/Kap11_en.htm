Areas of responsibility as elements of the system personnel planning (Hackstein 1971, p. 34) and methods employed (Oechsler 1981, p.5)

Planning of ascertainment of staff requirements

Within the framework of planning the ascertainment of staff requirement, we need to ascertain the long-, medium- and short-term ascertainment of staff requirement of different occupational groups in terms of quantity and quality (requirement profile) in all areas and sub-areas of responsibilty.

Planning of personnel recruitment

If staff requirement is permanently or temporarily exceeded, the necessary measures to meet this demand on the labour market - in the interests of hospital staff also internally - are taken within the framework of personnel recruitment

Planning of personnel development

Basically, there are two tasks for the personnel development of a hospital: on the one hand, adapting the skills of staff according to the demands, on the other hand, supporting each member of staff's interests geared towards personal development. Based on this duality of personnel development, we also speak of career planning.

Planning of staff employment

The task of planning staff employment is to best possibly integrate the available personnel capacities in the overall work process of the hospital, in terms of quantity, quality and time. The best possible integration implies that economical aspects need to be taken into account as evaluation criteria, while at the same time social aspects of individual members of staff need to be considered.

Planning of personnel maintenance

The task of planning personnel maintenance is to permanently ensure the personnel capacities of the hospital. This includes maintaining the capability as well the motivation of staff.

Planning of redundancy

The planning of redundancy is concerned with eliminating overstaffing in terms of quantity, quality, location, and time.

OPTIONS OF THE MODEL

  • favorisation of shift alternatives (e.g. varying duration of night shifts)
  • free planning of breaks through indication of recreation area
  • consideration of preferred break times
  • limitation of number of staff of a shift alternative
  • calculation of staff requirement for varying weekend working times (e.g. part time workers)
  • optional possibility of partly understaffing the profile ("filing off of ends of profile")
  • optional possibility of approaching the profile with the lowest sum of tolerances
  • assistance in searching for an integer solution 


The constraints

  We define

     S = the set of acceptable shift types
   Sed = the set of shifts which employee e is available to take on day d
    Ps = the number of quarter hour periods in shift s
  Xesd = I if employee e is assigned shift s on day d; 0 otherwise (decision variable)
   Aps = I if period p is a duty period for a schedule s; 0 otherwise
   Dpd = demand, in projected numbers of employees required to be on duty in period p of day d
   upd = goal programming deviation variable for falling short of the projected demand
   vpd = goal programming deviation variable for exceeding the projected demand
    Ue = maximum quarter-hour periods of work for employee e during the week
    Le = minimum quarter-hour periods of work for employee e during the week
    Ge = the desired (goal) minilllum quarter-hour periods of work for employee e during the week
    Ye = a goal programming deviation variable that allows employee e to work less than Ge periods
         if there is not enough work to go round.
